About This Project

This is a sample interactive report built to demonstrate survey data analysis skills. The dataset contains 10,247 simulated youth respondents across 330 questions covering demographics, mental wellbeing, access to support services, and education/employment status. The report is delivered as a navigable HTML interface with filterable bar charts and cross-tabulation breakdowns — replicating a real client deliverable format. Built with Python and Pandas for data processing, with a custom HTML/CSS/JS front-end for presentation.

AI Key Finding

Over half of respondents (53%) rated their mental wellbeing as poor or very poor, with female respondents significantly more likely to report low wellbeing (61%) compared to male respondents (42%). The 16–18 age group showed the most concerning results, with 67% reporting poor wellbeing — 14 percentage points above the survey average.

Q13: Access to Support

Section B · Multiple Choice · 9,988 valid responses

Section B · Wellbeing
Have you been able to access mental health support services when you needed them?
Q13 · Single choice · n = 9,988
Single Choice
✦
AI Key Finding

Only 29% of young people reported being able to access mental health support when they needed it. The most common barrier was long waiting times (cited by 44% of those who could not access support), followed by not knowing where to find help (31%). Access was notably worse in rural areas compared to urban respondents.
