Health Insurance Claims Analysis

Analysis of claim rejection patterns, billing trends, and complaint risk scoring across insurance providers and medical conditions using ~55,500 patient records.

Dataset: ~55,500 patient records
Variables: 15 fields
Source: Kaggle (CC0 Public Domain)
Python Pandas Matplotlib Seaborn ReportLab
Overview

Insurance claim rejections create friction for patients and administrative burden for providers. Understanding which conditions, insurers, and demographics are most affected is essential for identifying systemic issues and guiding process improvements.

This project applies feature engineering, statistical analysis, and data visualization to a public healthcare dataset to answer four core business questions — and synthesizes the findings into a composite complaint risk score and a comprehensive PDF report.

Research Questions
01
Which medical conditions and insurers have the highest claim rejection rates?
02
How do billing amounts vary across conditions, admission types, and age groups?
03
What variables most influence claim processing duration?
04
Which insurer–condition combinations present elevated complaint risk?
Engineered Features
claim_status (Approved / Rejected / Pending)
is_rejected (binary rejection indicator)
processing_days (length of stay proxy)
processing_band (categorical time grouping)
age_group (demographic brackets)
billing_band (cost categorization)
complaint_risk_score (composite metric)
