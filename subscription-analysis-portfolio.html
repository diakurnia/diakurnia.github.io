Subscription Digital Analysis

An end-to-end data analytics project transforming messy multi-source subscription data into customer segmentation, revenue insights, and actionable business recommendations.

Type: Data Analytics
Records: Multi-source CSV
Output: 6-sheet Excel Report
Python Pandas NumPy OpenPyXL
Overview

A digital subscription platform was exporting data from two separate systems — a CRM and an activity tracker — using different ID formats that made them impossible to join directly. This project demonstrates the full data analyst workflow: ingesting messy raw data, diagnosing and fixing quality issues, normalizing and merging the datasets, then extracting business intelligence through segmentation and trend analysis.

The result is a clean, audit-ready Excel workbook with six analysis sheets covering customer segmentation, revenue patterns, engagement metrics, and at-risk segment identification.

Analytical Workflow
1
Data Ingestion & Diagnosis Loaded customers.csv and activity.csv. Identified and documented inconsistent ID formats, ~5–8% missing values, ~3% duplicate records, mixed date formats, and product category typos.
2
Cleaning & Normalization Applied regex normalization to unify ID formats across both sources. Deduplicated records, standardized date parsing, corrected category typos, and removed ghost user IDs.
3
Multi-Source Merge Performed inner join on normalized customer IDs to reconcile CRM and activity data into a single unified dataset.
4
Segmentation & Analysis Segmented customers across 5 product categories. Analyzed revenue patterns, engagement metrics, cross-category behavior, and monthly trends.
5
Reporting Produced a 6-sheet Excel workbook with cleaning audit log, segment analysis, and an executive summary with recommendations.

Data Quality Issues Fixed

  • 5–8% missing values imputed or flagged
  • ~3% duplicate customer records removed
  • Mixed date formats standardized
  • Product category typos corrected
  • Ghost user IDs (no customer match) removed
  • Invalid email formats flagged
